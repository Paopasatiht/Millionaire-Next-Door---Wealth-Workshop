# MND Methodology

Core principles from *The Millionaire Next Door* translated into actionable workshop modules.

---

## The 4 Diagnostic Questions

These 4 questions are the foundation of the annual workshop. Answer them honestly before filling any spreadsheet.

### Q1 — Do you operate on an annual budget?

> "Prodigious Accumulators of Wealth (PAW) budget. Under-Accumulators of Wealth (UAW) do not."

- Yes/No is not enough — rate yourself: **None / Informal / Written / Tracked monthly**
- A budget that isn't tracked is just a wish list

### Q2 — Do you know how much your family spends each year on food, clothing, and shelter?

> Millionaires know exactly where money goes. They don't guess.

- Broken into sub-categories (see Annual Budget sheet)
- Goal: zero surprise categories by month 3

### Q3 — Do you have clearly defined goals for the next week, month, year, and decade?

> "Goals exist on a hierarchy. Lifetime goals cascade into annual, then monthly, then weekly."

- All 5 time horizons must be filled: daily, weekly, monthly, annual, lifetime
- Review quarterly — goals that don't get reviewed get abandoned

### Q4 — Do you spend a lot of time planning your financial future?

> PAWs spend significantly more time planning investments than UAWs.

- Benchmark: **8+ hours/month** on financial planning and investment study
- Log actual hours in Planning Time Log sheet

## PAW vs UAW Formula

Expected Net Worth = (Age × Annual Pre-tax Income) / 10

- **PAW:** net worth ≥ 2× expected
- **AAW:** net worth = 1× expected (average accumulator)
- **UAW:** net worth ≤ 0.5× expected

Recalculate this every January in the Dashboard sheet.

## Key Mindset Shifts

| UAW Pattern | PAW Pattern |
|---|---|
| High income, high lifestyle | High income, controlled lifestyle |
| Buys status symbols | Buys time and financial independence |
| No written budget | Written, tracked, reviewed budget |
| Vague financial goals | Specific goals with deadlines |
| Reacts to finances | Plans finances proactively |
